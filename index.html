General Caregiver Terms

  • ADLs (Activities of Daily Living) – Assisted the patient with basic tasks like bathing, dressing, grooming, etc.
  • Care Plan – Followed the patient’s care plan, ensuring all tasks were completed.
  • Transfer – Transferred patient from bed to wheelchair.
  • Respite Care – Temporary care provided while the primary caregiver took a break.

Medications

  • Med Pass – Administered the patient’s medication as prescribed at the appropriate time.
  • Pain Management – Provided pain relief medication at 6 PM.
  • Insulin Management – Administered insulin after dinner, monitored blood sugar.

Mobility

  • Ambulation – Assisted the patient in walking with a cane.
  • Transferred – Moved the patient from wheelchair to bed with a Hoyer lift.
  • Fall Risk Precautions – Fall precautions in place, no falls occurred today.

Nutrition & Hydration

  • Meals – Patient ate 75% of breakfast, encouraged more fluids.
  • Hydration – Encouraged patient to drink 8 oz of water every two hours.
  • Feeding Assistance – Provided assistance with eating and thickened liquids.

Incontinence & Toileting

  • Incontinence Care – Changed the patient's pad after bowel movement at 8:30 AM.
  • Toileted – Assisted the patient with toileting at regular intervals (every 2 hours).
  • Bowel Movement – The patient had a bowel movement, changed pad at 8 PM.

Examples of Use in Charting

  • 7:30 AM: Assisted the client with dressing and toileting.
  • 7:00 PM: Client ambulated with a cane for 10 minutes.
  • 8:00 PM: Changed the client’s diaper after a bowel movement, provided incontinence care.
  • 8:15 PM: Administered pain medication as prescribed.
  • 9:00 PM: Patient rested comfortably in bed, no complaints of pain.
